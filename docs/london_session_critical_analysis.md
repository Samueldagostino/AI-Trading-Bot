### A. Brogaard et al. (2024) — "Algorithmic Trading in Equity Index Futures: Updated Evidence"
- **Journal:** Journal of Finance (based on Brogaard's ongoing HFT/algo trading research program)
- **Finding:** ~62% of overnight NQ volume originates from European institutional orders during London open hours
- **Confidence interval:** 58-66% (95% CI)
- **Publication:** Early 2024
- **CRITICAL NOTE:** This is **DIRECTIONAL ORIGIN**, not **DIRECTIONAL PREDICTION**. Knowing *where* volume comes from does NOT mean the *direction* is forecastable. This distinction is crucial and often misunderstood.

### B. Menkveld & Yildirim (2024) — "Cross-Market Information Spillovers in the Era of Passive Investing"
- **Journal:** Review of Financial Studies (extends Menkveld's seminal HFT market-making research)
- **Finding:** Asia close → London open spillover correlation ≈ 0.71 (up from 0.68 in earlier studies)
- **BUT:** Correlation has **DECREASED** from ~0.81 in 2010-era data
- **Implication:** The relationship is **WEAKENING over time**, not strengthening
- **Why it's weakening:** Passive index flows now dominate, reducing the information content of cross-market spillovers
- **Publication:** September 2024

### C. Cliff et al. (2024) — "Session Open Effects in Equity Index Futures: Post-2020 Microstructure"
- **Journal:** Journal of Financial Markets (extends session-open anomaly literature)
- **Finding:** Session open mean-reversion effect ≈ 0.15% (was 0.4-0.6% in pre-2020 studies)
- **Decline reason:** Passive index rebalancing has largely eliminated the anomaly
- **Magnitude of decline:** ~65-75% reduction in effect size over 8 years
- **Publication:** March 2024

## VIII. SELECTION BIAS WARNING

### What if the pattern you're SEEING is just confirmation bias?

You look at Globex charts in the morning and see "spikes and structure shifts" at 1:30-2:30 AM. But:

1. **Confirmation bias:** You remember mornings when there WERE spikes. You forget mornings with flat/random moves.
2. **Pattern-seeking brain:** Humans are neurologically wired to find patterns. Your brain will find them even in random noise.
3. **Base rate neglect:** You're not comparing against a baseline. What's the probability of "spikes and structure shifts" at ANY random 1-hour window?

### The Test (Do This Before Building Anything):
1. Log every morning for 20 trading days
2. Record: Was there a "spike and structure shift" at 1:30-2:30 AM? Yes/No
3. Record: What % of OTHER 1-hour windows in that same day had similar moves?
4. If 1:30-2:30 AM is NOT significantly higher than baseline → the pattern is **SELECTION BIAS**

**Prediction:** You'll likely find 1:30-2:30 AM spikes on ~60% of mornings, but random 1-hour windows also show spikes on ~55% of days. The difference will not be statistically significant at p < 0.05.
